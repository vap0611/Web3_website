Core Web3 Concepts

Understanding Web3 becomes much easier when we compare important technologies side by side. These comparisons explain the fundamental concepts in a simple and practical way.

🌐

Web2 vs Web3

Web2Web3
Data is controlled by companies.Users own and control their data.
Login with email and password.Login using a crypto wallet.
Applications run on centralized servers.Applications run on decentralized blockchain networks.
Payments require banks or intermediaries.Peer-to-peer transactions without intermediaries.
Companies can modify or remove user data.Blockchain records are transparent and difficult to alter.
Quick Summary: "Web2 focuses on centralized services, while Web3 focuses on ownership, transparency, and decentralization."
🪙

Bitcoin vs Ethereum

BitcoinEthereum
Designed mainly as digital money.Designed for smart contracts and decentralized applications.
Uses BTC as its native currency.Uses ETH as its native currency.
Limited scripting capabilities.Supports programmable smart contracts.
Primarily used as a store of value.Used for DeFi, NFTs, DAOs, and Web3 applications.
Focuses on secure value transfer.Focuses on building decentralized ecosystems.
Quick Summary: "Bitcoin is digital money, while Ethereum is a programmable blockchain platform."
🔑

Public Key vs Private Key

Public KeyPrivate Key
Can be shared with anyone.Must always remain secret.
Used to receive cryptocurrency.Used to sign and authorize transactions.
Similar to a bank account number.Similar to an ATM PIN or password.
Does not give access to funds.Gives complete control over the wallet.
Safe to display publicly.Never share with anyone.
Quick Summary: "The public key identifies your wallet, while the private key proves ownership and secures your assets."
🗄️

Traditional Database vs Blockchain

Traditional DatabaseBlockchain
Controlled by a single organization.Shared across many computers.
Data can be edited or deleted.Data is nearly impossible to modify after recording.
Faster for centralized applications.Prioritizes transparency and security.
Trust depends on one authority.Trust is established through decentralized consensus.
Commonly used in banking and business software.Used in cryptocurrencies, NFTs, DeFi, and Web3 applications.
Quick Summary: "Traditional databases rely on central control, whereas blockchain creates trust through decentralization and immutable records."
